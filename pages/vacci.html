Understanding the Vaccines We Use

Rabies Vaccine

A crucial vaccine providing immunity against the deadly rabies virus, essential for public health and animal welfare.

DHLPP Vaccine

A combination vaccine protecting against Distemper, Hepatitis, Leptospirosis, Parainfluenza, Parvovirus, and Coronavirus.

Bordetella Vaccine

Targets kennel cough, a highly contagious respiratory disease common among dogs in close proximity.

Our Vaccination Process

From identification to post-care monitoring, every step is carefully planned

1

Location Scouting

Our team identifies areas with high stray dog populations through community reports and field surveys.

2

Dog Capture & Assessment

Trained professionals safely capture dogs and perform health assessments to determine vaccination needs.

3

Vaccination Administration

Licensed veterinarians administer vaccines following proper medical protocols and safety procedures.

4

Recovery & Monitoring

Dogs are monitored for immediate reactions and released. Follow-up tracking ensures continued health.

5

Documentation & Tracking

Each dog receives identification and vaccination records maintained for future healthcare planning.

Safety & Post-Vaccination Care

Safe Handling Protocols

Our trained professionals use humane capture methods and stress-reduction techniques to ensure dog safety and comfort.

Medical Monitoring

Each dog is monitored for 30 minutes post-vaccination for any immediate reactions before release back to their territory.

Identification Tags

Vaccinated dogs receive colored ear tags or collars for easy identification and to track vaccination status in future encounters.

Emergency Protocols

Emergency veterinary support is always available during drives, with immediate treatment protocols for any complications.
